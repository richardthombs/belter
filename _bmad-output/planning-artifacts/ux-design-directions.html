Direction A

Ultra-minimal HUD

Only credits, sector ID, and cargo bar persist. Everything else is on-demand. The universe breathes in open space.

Direction A

Ghost panel

Context panel fades in from the right edge with no hard chrome frame — gradient fade, borderless. Very lightweight visual presence.

Direction A

Best for

Players who want to feel alone in space. Maximises the "empty void" emotional goal. May feel too sparse for new players.

Direction B — Navigator Structured chrome

A bottom HUD bar grounds the experience with always-visible stats. Context panel has clear chrome framing. Feels more like a serious tool alongside the world.

Direction B

Persistent bottom bar

Credits, hold, speed, and sector always visible. Grounds the player with always-present context without cluttering the canvas centre.

Direction B

Framed panel chrome

Context panel has a clear header/footer structure — title, info rows, action buttons. Feels more like a precision instrument than a floating overlay.

Direction B

Best for

Players who want clear information hierarchy. Easier to scan quickly. The bottom bar provides constant orientation. Slightly more "HUD" feel.

Direction C — Prospector Warm & mercantile

Left sidebar for navigation actions. Bottom sheet for context panels (slides up from below). Cargo and credits more prominent. Feels like a working miner's tool.

Direction C

Left sidebar + bottom sheet

Sidebar holds persistent nav actions. Context slides up as a bottom sheet — very natural on mobile, familiar from Maps/Apple apps.

Direction C

Credits & hold prominent

Top bar makes economic status immediately visible. Hold shown as pip indicators — at a glance, not a number. More "merchant" feel.

Direction C

Best for

Players who identify as miners or traders first. The bottom sheet pattern is the most mobile-native of the three. Highest touch ergonomics score.
